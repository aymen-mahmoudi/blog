Einstein's Riddle

Although that there isn’t any solid evidence to confirm that Einstein created it, the story goes that Einstein wrote this complicated logic puzzle when he was a young man, and he estimated that only 2 Percent of People Can solve it.

Here’s the setup: There are five houses, each painted a different color. In each house lives a person with a different nationality. These five owners drink a certain type of beverage, smoke a certain brand of cigar, and keep a certain pet. No owners have the same pet, smoke the same cigar, or drink the same beverage.
We need to identify who owns the fish, and we have the 15 following clues:

  1. The Brit lives in the red house
  2. The Swede keeps dogs as pets
  3. The Dane drinks tea
  4. The green house is on the left of the white house
  5. The green house’s owner drinks coffee
  6. The person who smokes Pall Mall rears birds
  7. The owner of the yellow house smokes Dunhill
  8. The man living in the center house drinks milk
  9. The Norwegian lives in the first house
  10. The man who smokes blend has a neighbor who drinks water
  11. The Norwegian lives next to the blue house
  12. The man who smokes blends lives next to the one who keeps cats
  13. The man who keeps horses lives next to the man who smokes Dunhill
  14. The owner who smokes BlueMaster drinks beer
  15. The German smokes Prince

The moving sofa problem

This problem was posed by the Austrian mathematician Leo Moser in 1966 and it still unsolved. The problem is the following: what is the shape of largest area in the plane that can be moved around a right-angled corner in a two-dimensional hallway of width 1?
